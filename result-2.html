Where This Goes Without Intervention

Pre-diabetes isn’t a warning. It’s the beginning.

Most people hear “pre-diabetic” and think they have time. They adjust a few things, lose a few pounds, and figure they’ve handled it. Then the number creeps up again. Then it doesn’t come back down.

The research is consistent: without directly addressing insulin resistance, the majority of pre-diabetics will progress to full Type 2 diabetes within 10 years. For many, it happens in 5. The standard advice — “watch your diet, exercise more, we’ll check again in six months” — has a well-documented track record of not working.

This isn’t a scare tactic. It’s the documented trajectory of unaddressed insulin resistance. The pattern you’re experiencing right now — the crashes, the cravings, the belly fat that won’t move — is the same pattern that appears in lab work 3 to 5 years before a formal diagnosis.
